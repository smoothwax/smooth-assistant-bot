## About Smooth Wax Bar

Smooth Wax Bar is a waxing salon with locations in London, Ontario and Toronto (Liberty Village). It is dedicated to waxing services, including Brazilians, backs, brows, toes, and everything in between. Smooth uses luxury imported waxes and trained Waxologists. The brand promise is fast, hygienic, virtually ouch-free waxing at a fair price.

Open since April 2014 in London and October 2015 in Toronto.

**Brand language / tone:**
- Get Smooth.
- Maximum smoothness with Minimum ouch.
- Be Smooth. Feel Fabulous. Fast.
- Team Smooth.
- Waxologists.
- Smooth Packs.
- Club Smooth Memberships.

**Why Smooth (four pillars):**
1. Less Ouch — Waxologists are specially trained in techniques that reduce discomfort.
2. Cleanliness — highly focused on hygiene; Waxologists never double dip wax sticks.
3. Priced Right — high-quality waxing without luxury spa pricing.
4. Fast, Quality Service — Waxologists specialize in waxing and aim to provide quick, high-quality service.

**Wax types:**
- Hard wax: used on sensitive body parts (facial, underarm, intimate areas). Made of natural beeswax and polymers, with titanium dioxide, Micro Mia, Argan Oil, Arnica, and Aloe Vera. Shrink-wraps around the hair rather than the skin, so it's less painful and doesn't remove a layer of skin.
- Strip wax: used on larger body areas (legs, arms, chest, back). Contains Coconut Oil and Titanium Dioxide. Applied as a thin layer with a cotton strip on top; grips the skin and can remove a thin layer of skin with it.

For services over 1 hour in duration, a 10% gratuity is automatically added.

## Reception Procedures

### Reception team mission
You are the first person the client sees when they enter and the last person they interact with when they leave. Smile, welcome the client on arrival, ensure they leave satisfied. Same goes for phone calls — professional, happy voice tone.

### Reception musts
- Friendly, polite, and kind. Show empathy and caring. Professional and courteous.
- Accommodate clients to the best of your ability; approach issues as "client is always right."
- Diffuse customer frustration by showing empathy.
- Keep reception area neat and tidy — straighten magazines, keep the desk clutter-free, dust product shelves.
- Dress is smart casual. Minimal personal phone usage.
- All front-desk conversations must be professional — save personal chats with coworkers for when clients aren't present; voices carry even when a client is in a service room.
- The vibe at Smooth is intentionally casual but always professional.

### Answering the phone
"Good morning/afternoon/evening, Smooth Wax Bar, [your name], speaking." If busy with a client at the desk, still answer and ask the caller to hold — never let it ring unanswered.

### Booking an appointment
1. Ask if they've been in before — if so, get their phone number to find their profile.
2. Ask what service(s) they want and their preference on date/time/Waxologist.
3. If the client wants a specific Waxologist, click the pink star next to the name when booking so the appointment can't be moved.
4. If the client wants to ONLY ever see one Waxologist, add a booking alert to their file (customer file → Details → scroll to booking alert).
5. Try to book with no dead space between appointments — offer the earlier open slot if it works for the client.
6. If you can't click the exact slot, click a nearby one and adjust the time manually in the dropdowns.
7. For multiple services, click "+ Add Another Service" and repeat.
8. If nothing is available at the desired time, offer the waitlist.

### Moving an appointment
- **Same day:** Right-click → Edit → adjust time manually. If Booker won't let you move a multi-service appointment, move one service at a time, hitting "update appointment" and refreshing between each, until the whole appointment has moved with no gaps.
- **Different day, same Waxologist:** Right-click → "Move to clipboard" → go to new date → drag to the desired time (appears bottom-left of screen). Repeat for additional services.
- **Different day, different Waxologist:** Booker doesn't support this directly. Right-click → Cancel → "Early Cancel – No Charge," then rebook fresh.

### Client running late
Call to check if they're still coming or want to reschedule. If unreachable and there's no longer enough time, mark "No Show" after attempting to reach them. Clients with multiple services booked have more wiggle room — check with the Waxologist about what's feasible in the remaining time.

### No shows
Wait until at least halfway into the appointment before marking "No Show" (e.g., 10+ minutes late for a Brazilian). Try to contact the client first. Right-click → No-Show → "No-Show – Don't Charge." Booker auto-emails the client. After a 2nd no-show, a manual email goes out warning that a 3rd no-show requires a credit card on file. After the 3rd no-show, cancel any future appointment and send the no-show email (template below) explaining a credit card is required to hold appointments going forward.

### Cancelling an appointment
- More than 2 hours before: right-click → Cancel → "Early cancel – No Charge."
- Less than 2 hours before: right-click → Cancel → "Late Cancel – No Charge."

### Checking in a client
Right-click their appointment → "Check-In Guest." New clients fill out a New Client form — both reception and the Waxologist should scan it for allergies, products used, or activities affecting the skin. Any allergies or "yes" answers go into the client's profile: appointment → client name → Details → "Check-In Alert" → save.

**Undoing a check-in:** if mistakenly checked in or "Take Payment/Check Out" was pressed in error, right-click → "Undo Check-In."

## Glossary

- **Sulfa drug:** A group of drugs that are a very common allergy.
- **Accutane:** An oral acne medication that thins the skin everywhere — clients cannot be waxed within 6 months of taking it.
- **Retin-A / Glycolic:** Topical acne treatments that thin skin; waxing cannot be done where these products have been applied.
- **Derivative:** Something derived from a larger group — e.g., ragweed allergies imply chamomile allergies, since chamomile is a ragweed derivative.
- **Hard Wax:** Used for facial, underarm, and intimate waxing. A thick, viscous wax removed by itself (no cotton strip); shrink-wraps around the hair rather than the skin, so it's less painful and doesn't strip a layer of skin.
- **Strip Wax:** The more familiar wax, applied thinly with a cotton backing; used on legs, arms, chest, back. Grips the skin and can take a thin layer of skin with it.
- **Labia:** The inner and outer folds of the vulva. Relevant for bikini waxing — Brazilians include this area; sexy and classic bikinis do not.
- **pH balance:** A measure of acidity/alkalinity; many products are discouraged for altering the body's natural pH.
- **Folliculitis:** The most common cause of an allergic-reaction-like symptom — infection/inflammation in the hair follicles.
- **Epilate:** To remove hair (often associated with epilator machines).
- **Dermabrasion / Microdermabrasion:** A deep-exfoliating cosmetic treatment that removes skin layers — makes waxing a bad idea afterward.
- **Salicylic acid:** A chemical exfoliant commonly used for acne treatment.
- **Acetylsalicylic acid:** Aspirin — used for pain, fever, and inflammation.
- **Dry brushing:** An exfoliation method with circulation and lymphatic benefits.
- **Exfoliate:** Removing dead skin via dry brushing, scrubs, or chemical exfoliants.
- **Jute:** A fibrous plant material from the inner bark of the sisal plant.
- **Sisal:** A rougher version of the same fibrous plant material used to make jute.

## Client-Facing FAQs

**Waxing during your period?** Yes, as long as you're comfortable and wear a tampon. You may be more sensitive than usual, especially in the days just before your cycle.

**How long before vacation should I wax?** Recommend 24 hours between waxing and going in pools/water or tanning, for redness to settle.

**Pre-wax tips for first-timers:** Some clients take a painkiller beforehand; a warm shower can help open pores. Staff should phrase this as "some clients like to," never as a direct recommendation to take medication.

**How long will the wax last?** Varies by hair growth and type — most clients return every 3-6 weeks (4 being most common). Smooth, hair-free skin typically lasts about 2 weeks if hair growth was even; occasional early regrowth is normal due to different hair-cycle stages or exfoliation revealing ingrowns.

**How long does hair need to be?** Recommend 1/4–1/3 inch (about the length of a grain of rice). Facial hair can sometimes be shorter. Clients with hair shorter than this for body waxing may be turned away.

**Does it hurt?** Pain tolerance varies. Most regulars feel little to no discomfort — Smooth uses high-quality imported hard wax that shrink-wraps the hair rather than pulling at the skin. Painkillers or numbing cream are options for the worried.

**Side effects?** Some tenderness, redness, and inflammation that typically resolves within 24-72 hours.

**Why avoid Retin-A/glycolic/Accutane?** These thin the skin. Retin-A/glycolic are topical, so waxing is avoided only where applied. Accutane is oral and thins skin everywhere, so no waxing at all while taking it (within 6 months).

**Allergy concerns:** Smooth's wax contains beeswax, aloe, tea tree, and other ingredients — service can be tailored if a client has a known allergy.

**Why no tanning 24 hours before/after?** Tanning then waxing risks infection; waxing then tanning increases burn risk. Waxing burnt skin also simply hurts more.

**Why are men's prices higher?** Larger treatment areas and more product/time typically required; men also tend to have more hair.

**What to wear for intimate waxing?** Full Brazilian, Modest Brazilian, or Brozilian require removing everything from the waist down. For sexy or classic bikini, clients can keep their own bottoms on.

**What wax is used where?** Hard wax for facial, underarm, and intimate areas (with a little strip wax on sparse-hair bikini-area lines). Strip wax for everything else (legs, arms, chest, back).

**What to do in between appointments?** Nothing extra needed — frequent waxing itself improves results over time. Light tweezing of stray hairs is fine in the first few days. Free touch-ups are offered within a week of service.

**Will hair grow back thinner?** Many clients see thinner regrowth over time, since waxing pulls hair from the root (unlike shaving, which reinforces the root by cutting mid-shaft).

**Test strips:** Available on request — useful for sensitivity, allergy, or past-bad-experience concerns. Reception should book 5-10 minutes max, no charge.

**Can someone accompany a client into the room?** No, except a parent with a small child (or vice versa) — rooms are small.

**Student discount?** No standard student discount, but an 8-month student Brazilian membership is available.

### Other FAQs

**Laser vs. waxing:** Waxing is more affordable. Laser works best for light skin/dark hair combinations and can burn darker skin (it targets dark pigment). Laser also isn't fully permanent — 30-40% regrowth is possible, requiring multiple costly treatments.

**Male clients:** ~30% of Smooth's clientele. Reasons include back/chest hair removal, athletic performance, and brow grooming. The environment is unisex and Waxologists are trained for male clients.

**Ingrown hairs:** Waxologists won't break the skin to remove ingrowns (only medical professionals are certified to). Smooth can advise on prevention.

**When NOT to wax:** Raised moles, varicose veins, acne medication use (Accutane, Retin-A), corticosteroid use. Staff should escalate unclear contraindication/medication questions to a manager.

**Missed spot or asymmetry after a wax:** Contact Smooth within 48 hours and they'll get the client back in to fix it.

**Why does it smell so good?** A combination of the wax used and product testers in the space.
